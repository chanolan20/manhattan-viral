# Manhattan Viral — Imagery Direction

## Brand idea

Manhattan Viral is not a studio brand. It is shot on the street, in the block, on the rooftop, in the bodega. Imagery should feel **documentary, not catalog** — as if a local photographer followed the crew for a day.

---

## Photography direction

### Subject matter

- Real people wearing the product in NYC environments
- Street corners, subway entrances, rooftops, bodegas, project hallways, Dominican neighborhoods
- Detail shots: fabric texture, puff print, embroidery, tags, packaging
- Candid moments: laughing, walking, waiting, smoking, talking
- Product flat lays on concrete, newsprint, fire escapes, checkerboard floors

### Composition

- **Tight crops** for product detail and emotion
- **Wide environment** shots to establish place
- **Asymmetric framing** — never perfectly centered unless intentional
- **Layered depth** — foreground subject, mid-ground action, background city texture

### Lighting

- Natural daylight, slightly overcast or golden hour
- Hard shadows acceptable if they feel like street light or late-afternoon sun
- Avoid flat studio lighting
- Night shots should use available light: bodega glow, street lamps, neon

### Color treatment

- **True color with warm lift** — skin tones should feel alive
- Slight desaturation of greens and blues so gold/orange brand accents pop
- High contrast, crushed blacks acceptable for mood
- Avoid faded vintage filters; keep it present-tense

### What to reject

- Stock-photo models with generic smiles
- White seamless studio backgrounds
- Overly posed influencer aesthetics
- Heavy vintage/sepia filters
- AI-generated people that look synthetic

---

## Illustration direction

Manhattan Viral does not use illustration as a primary language. When illustration is needed:

- **Style:** Minimal line work, single weight, geometric
- **Color:** Brand colors only (gold/orange/white on black)
- **Subject:** NYC landmarks reduced to simple silhouettes; subway lines; borough maps
- **Line treatment:** Clean outlines, no shading

---

## Iconography

See `iconography/` for the full icon set. At a glance:

- **Style:** Outline, 1.5px stroke, rounded caps
- **Grid:** 24×24 base, 16×16 for compact UI
- **Color:** White or gold on dark; black or orange on light
- **No filled icons** except for status indicators (cart count, badges)

---

## Video / motion stills

- 5–10 second seamless loops
- Focus on texture: fabric moving, print close-up, zipper pull, laces
- Keep movement small and physical — real product, not 3D render
- For social: 9:16 vertical, shot on phone if authentic
